7. A module-qualified struct literal does not parse. `q.holder { value: 7 }` after `use p.q` reports "expected end of line after this" pointing at `holder`, so the brace form is only reachable through an unqualified name. The *type* path (`q.holder` in an annotation) resolves, and a member import (`use p.q.holder`) gives a working spelling, so this is a parser gap rather than a resolution one — `parse_postfix` does not consider a `{` after a dotted path to open a struct literal. Found while fixing the qualified-type-path gap; the two are adjacent but independent.
8. Shouldn't tuples store their members like structs instead of a uniform 8-byte slot?
8. `break` and `continue` parse but do not lower. Both are documented in `spec/kira-reference.md` §"`break` and `continue`", and a loop using either type-checks and then fails with "no concrete checked type is available for this node" pointing at the keyword. The workaround is to restructure around a flag or an early `return`, which is what `std.algo`'s `flatten`/`flat_map` do — their single outer draw wants a `break` and uses an `if let` instead. Found while writing the nesting adapters.
9. An inherent method on a generic type cannot be called when the receiver's type argument is itself an unbound parameter of the enclosing `impl`. `self.cur.is_none()` where `cur: option[J]` inside `impl[I, J, T] ... for flatten_iter[I, J, T]` reports "cannot tell which `T` this call to `is_none` means", even though `extend[T] option[T]` does mention `T` in its target type — matching `option[T]` against `option[J]` should solve it. Worked around by restructuring to avoid the call.
